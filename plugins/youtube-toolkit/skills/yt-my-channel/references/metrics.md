# Metrics interpretation — IT / vibe-coding niche

## Engagement ratios — rough targets

For a Russian-language IT channel, healthy public ratios on a video > 30 days old:

| Metric | Floor | Median | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Likes / views | 2.0% | 3.5% | 5.0%+ |
| Comments / views | 0.3% | 0.7% | 1.5%+ |
| (Likes + comments) / views | 2.5% | 4.5% | 6.5%+ |

If a video sits below the floor, it usually means the topic missed the audience or the title pulled the wrong viewer (high views but low engagement = clickbait without payoff).

## Views skew across age

Newer videos accumulate views over time. When comparing videos:

- **< 14 days old:** exclude from "underperformer" lists — too early.
- **14–90 days:** primary signal window.
- **> 1 year:** evergreen if still pulling weekly views; otherwise topical (peaked then died).

A video is **evergreen** if last-30-day views ≥ 30% of first-30-day views. Otherwise topical.

## Length signal

For an IT niche with planned 1 video/week up to 20 min, the read is:

- If top videos cluster at one length and most uploads at another — the channel is misaligned. Cut length toward where engagement lives.
- If top videos are notably shorter than the median upload — suspect over-padding.
- If top videos are notably longer — suspect insufficient depth in shorter content.

## Topic clusters

Tag each of the last 20 videos as one of: **flagship-series** (Logos / CRM / other named series), **tutorial** (one-off how-to), **lead-gen** (case study, agency-style, behind-the-scenes), **experiment**.

Compute view share per cluster. The cluster with the highest view share but lowest upload share is under-fed — recommend more of it.

## Red flags

- **Likes/views < 1.0% and > 1k views** — the title overpromised.
- **Comments/views = 0** on a 5k+ view video — disengaged audience or poor CTA to comment.
- **Top video is from > 1 year ago and nothing since has touched it** — channel drifted away from its strongest topic; investigate why.
- **Upload gaps > 3 weeks** — algorithm momentum lost; flag in Recommendations.

## When Studio data is pasted

Studio adds two metrics not available via API key:

- **Impressions click-through rate (CTR):** healthy for IT niche is 4–8% on average. Below 4% — thumbnail and/or title problem. Above 10% — usually the audience already knows you (high subscriber concentration).
- **Average view duration / Average percentage viewed:** below 30% = retention problem (intro too long, value delayed, or topic mismatch). 40–55% is solid.

Combine with public metrics: a video with high CTR but low retention pulled the wrong viewer; a video with low CTR but high retention has a thumbnail problem.
