# Thumbnail patterns for IT / vibe-coding channel

Five repeatable layouts that work for this niche. Pick by content type.

## Pattern A — Face + screen split

Left half: author's face (clear emotion). Right half: a screenshot of the build / IDE / interface, partially blurred or with one element highlighted.

- Best for: case studies, builds, opinion videos.
- Emotion candidates: surprise, "really?", focused.
- Text: 3 words, top-left or bottom-centre.
- Why it works: face delivers personality, screen delivers proof.

## Pattern B — Outcome-only

The finished thing fills the canvas. UI of the built app, the working ассистент, or the IDE in a striking moment.

- Best for: case studies where the outcome is visually impressive (a polished CRM dashboard, a working voice assistant interface).
- Emotion: implicit "look at this".
- Text: 2–3 words, one corner. Often a tag-style label ("CRM. 2 дня.").
- Why it works: the proof is the picture.

## Pattern C — Comparison

Two halves with contrast. Left: "before" or tool A. Right: "after" or tool B. Often with a small "vs" or arrow between.

- Best for: tool reviews, before/after builds.
- Emotion: tension.
- Text: short label per side or single word centre ("vs", "→", "ИЛИ").
- Why it works: the pattern is recognisable and triggers a comparison reflex in the viewer.

## Pattern D — Bold text dominates

A short sharp phrase fills 60–70% of the canvas. The image fills the remaining 30–40% as background context.

- Best for: opinion videos, hot takes.
- Emotion: confrontation, certainty.
- Text: 3–4 words. Sharp. ("Не используй", "Это сломалось", "Я ошибся").
- Why it works: the text *is* the hook.

## Pattern E — Series stamp

A consistent series-specific layout used across all episodes of Logos / CRM / etc. Think: episode number large, series name in a corner, central image of the episode's main artefact.

- Best for: every flagship-series episode.
- Emotion: continuity, recognition.
- Text: episode number + 2–3 word episode promise. ("ЛОГОС #7 — Память.")
- Why it works: regular viewers spot the series instantly. New viewers see "this is part of a series — there's a backstory" and click.

The series stamp must be a consistent template. Once defined for episode 1, freeze the colour, font, position. Variation kills the recognition value.

## Selection rule

| Video type | Default pattern | Backup |
|---|---|---|
| Tutorial | A (face + screen) | D (bold text) |
| Case study | B (outcome-only) | A (face + screen) |
| Tool review | C (comparison) | D (bold text) |
| Flagship episode | E (series stamp) | — |
| Lead-gen anchor | B (outcome-only) | A (face + screen) |

For flagship episodes, never substitute pattern E with another — series consistency is the long-game CTR lever.

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Multi-text overlay (more than one phrase). The eye doesn't know where to look.
- Excessive emojis or emoji-as-text.
- Red circles around things. Saturated, signals low quality.
- Random gradients with no anchor.
- Stock photos of "developer at laptop". Generic kills.
- Yellow MrBeast-style arrows. Wrong audience for this niche.
