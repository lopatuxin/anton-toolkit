```markdown
# Competitor report — @handle

## 1. Snapshot
- Subscribers: <N>
- Total views: <N>
- Total videos: <N>
- Created: <YYYY-MM-DD>
- Avg views per video (last 12): <N>
- Posting cadence (last 12 weeks): <X videos/week>
- Avg engagement (likes/views) on last 12: <X.X%>

## 2. Top 10 videos (by views)
| # | Title | Views | Likes/views | Length | Published |
|---|-------|-------|-------------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | … | … | …% | m:ss | YYYY-MM-DD |
…

## 3. Direction (last 10 uploads)
- Active topic clusters: <list 2–4>
- Whether they shifted from earlier top videos: <yes/no, evidence>
- New formats they are testing: <if any>

## 4. Hook formulas
Extract 3–5 reusable hook patterns from top titles. Format each as a template, e.g.:
- "Я сделал <X> за <Y> дней с помощью <tool>" — used N times
- "Почему я больше не использую <tool>" — used N times
- "<Tool> vs <Tool>: <verdict>" — used N times

## 5. Length and pacing
- Median duration of top 10: <m:ss>
- Median duration of last 10 uploads: <m:ss>
- Mismatch? <if top differs from current — note>

## 6. Tags and topics
- Top tag overlaps across the top 10 (count): <list>
- Topic clusters and view share

## 7. What works for them (hypotheses)
2–3 hypotheses with evidence pointing back to specific videos.

## 8. What we can borrow
3–5 concrete hooks/formats/length-decisions worth testing on our channel — without copying the topic 1:1.

## 9. What to avoid copying
2–3 things specific to their audience or branding that would not transfer.
```

## Writing rules

- Numbers exact, copied from MCP responses.
- Quote titles verbatim (Russian).
- Length cap: ~700 words.
- "Hypothesis" label on every claim about *why* something worked.
- "What to avoid" must be present — copy-paste of competitor strategy is a failure mode, not an output.
